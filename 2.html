Tips for Choosing the Right Course

We have compiled some suggestions for choosing the course. Here are some things that candidates must do before taking admission into any course. Consider these points before you choose any course:
Deciding the Aim: This is the best way of choosing any course. Moreover, those who have any career goal or aim, they know even before class 12th what they want to do. Even if you haven’t made any clear aim before class 12th, make one now. Having a goal will show you the right path and will also shortlist the options. We suggest students decide on their goals in class 12th only, if not before.
Consider your Interests: Consider your interests and make a list of your likings i.e. the subjects which you like and want to study in-depth. When you will shortlist the subjects, you can look for the courses in those particular subjects. Make a list of 10 to 15 options which matches with your interest. Don’t hesitate to include subjects which you haven’t studied in class 12th but are interested in pursuing. You just need to do a little study about these subjects (to check if they interest you).
Analysis : Now that you have shortlisted 10 to 15 options, analyse each option in your list thoroughly. Check for the facts like eligibility for the course, entrance exams/ admission process, top colleges, drawbacks, opportunities, growth, salary, etc related to each of the options. You can make a pros and cons list for each course to make a better decision.
Shortlist Again: Now considering the pros and cons of different options, shortlist your options again.
Some Practical Knowledge: After shortlisting 4 to 5 options or even less, get some practical knowledge about the courses and career from the one who are pursuing the course or working in the field. You can also check out videos from professionals about their daily work routine, if available. Practical knowledge will give you more clarity. For eg, if you want to become a lawyer, you can visit a local lawyer and ask him/ her about their job profile, what the profession demands, etc. Ask them if you can spend some time with them to understand things better.
These points do require numerous efforts but putting the efforts will be worth it if you will find the right course. We hope these points will help you in making an informed decision.
